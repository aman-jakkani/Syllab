About this course

This course is a follow-on to M470 and will both cover additional topics as well as go over some of the previous ones, but in more depth and sophistication. Having previously taken M470 is a de facto requirement. The only exception is a student who has taken all the background mathematics which could partially compensate for lack of M470; this would consist of M415 and M427. Examples of new topics will include : Introduction to Information Theory, entropy and redundacy, in particular Error Correcting Codes. Deterministic vs probabilistic routines. Other public key cryptosystems beyond RSA, in particular, Elliptic curve cryptosystems and methods Digital cash (e,g. Bitcoin). Introduction to quantum computing and why it is important cryptographically. Examples of "old" topics revisited are How do we actually obtain random numbers? More on the structure/distribution of prime numbers, and finding roots of polynomial equations modulo n (and why from a cryptographic viewpoint one should care). The latest in factoring algorithms (and in fact updates on many others).
